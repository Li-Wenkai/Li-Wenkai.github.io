Wenkai Li (李文楷)

Hi! I am a final-year undergrad in Astronomy at the USTC and currently a visiting student at the Steward Observatory. For details, consult my CV.

My journey began as an amateur astronomer, culminating in a shortlisting by the Astronomy Photographer of the Year1 and solidifying research ambition. My goal is to become a “versatile astronomer,” skilled in observation, theory, simulation and instrument.

My research is mentored by Prof. Ning Jiang in Prof. Tinggui Wang’s research group at USTC, and by Prof. Ann Zabludoff at Steward Observatory.

Research:

  • Tidal Disruption Events & Intermediate-Mass Black Holes: EP240222a Model, Ansky as a TDE
  • Post-Starburst Galaxies: dPSBs
  • Survey Forecasts: EP Termal TDE Detection Rate, TDE & FBOT’s LF & Detection Rate
  • Astronomy Tools: USTC-astrXiv (蜗壳天文科研周报)
  • Active Galactic Nuclei: MCMC in AGN & Cosmology
  • For a deeper dive, please see my Research.
